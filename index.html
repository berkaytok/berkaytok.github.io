About

My work explores the intersection of computational systems and electroacoustic composition, with a particular focus on the emergent patterns that arise from the interaction between algorithmic processes and organic sound materials. Drawing from my background in sound engineering, spatial data science, and intermedia arts, I investigate how polyrhythmic structures can serve as frameworks for organizing both sonic and visual elements in responsive environments. My current research focuses on fine-tuning open source diffusion models for generative music and sound composition, allowing natural phenomena to influence the evolution of computer-generated soundscapes. Through this integration of technology and artistic practice, I seek to challenge traditional boundaries between data, sound, and space, creating immersive experiences that reflect the complex rhythms and patterns found in both natural and artificial systems.
